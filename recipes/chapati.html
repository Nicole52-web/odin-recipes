Kenyan Soft-Layered Chapati

To make a soft layered chapati certain ingredients and techniques are required for the success of cooking a good chapati kenyan style.

Some of the things you need are flour,salt,sugar,warm water and cooking oil. It roughly takes 1 hour to make.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups of flour
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • 3-5 tsp of oil
  • 1.75 cup of warm water
  • 6-8 tsp of butte/ghee
  • extra flour for dusting
  • 6 tsp of oil for roasting

Steps

  1. Add flour, salt and sugar in a mixing bowl. Mix well.
  2. Add 3-4 tbsp oil and rub into the flour.
  3. Add the measured warm water and form a soft dough. Add any extra water required, tablespoon at a time as you don't want the dough too soft.
  4. The dough should not be sticky.
  5. Take a tbsp of oil and knead it into the dough.
  6. Cover the dough with a damp cloth, lid or cling film. Allow the dough to rest for 30 minutes.
  7. Divide the dough into six parts. Roll each part into a ball.
  8. Cover the balls with a damp cloth. Allow them to rest for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Dust the worktop with some flour.
  10. Roll one dough ball out into a circle about 6-7 inches in diameter.
  11. Smear about 1 tsp solid ghee or butter all over the rolled dough.
  12. Sprinkle a bit of flour all over.
  13. Fold the rolled dough into a fan shape. Check out the video.
  14. Then roll it into a spiral shape.
  15. Allow the dough to rest for 10 minutes. In the meantime can roll and shape the remaining 5 dough balls.
  16. Dust the worktop with some flour.
  17. Take one of the spiral dough and roll it out flat into a circle of about 6-7 inches in diameter.
  18. Heat a griddle, tawa or frying pan over medium heat.
  19. Place the rolled out chapati over the griddle. Allow it roast for 1-2 minutes.
  20. Flip it over and allow the other side to roast for 1-2 minutes.
  21. Smear about 1 tsp oil, ghee or butter all over the top side. Flip it over.
  22. Roast or cook till brown specks appear.
  23. Smear oil, ghee or butter on the top dry side.
  24. Flip and roast. Roast till the chapati is done. Should see brown specks all over the chapati and some of the layers should come apart.
  25. Repeat the same with the remaining dough.
  26. Serve hot Kenyan Chapati with your favourite curry or stew. Or serve it hot with some tea.
